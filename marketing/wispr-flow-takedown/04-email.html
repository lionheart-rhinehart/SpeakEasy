Everyone in the group's hyping Wispr Flow. I ran the math — and didn't switch.

Quick one, because a few of you asked. Wispr Flow is a genuinely good dictation app — it turns your voice into text at your cursor. I tested it hard against the tool I actually run, SpeakEasy, and two things jumped out:

1. Wispr only types. SpeakEasy does things.

Press a hotkey, say "send to my newsletter Zap," "rewrite this as a hook," or "open Canva" — SpeakEasy fires the webhook, runs the AI prompt, or opens the tool. Wispr has zero automation. It's dictation, full stop.

2. The cost isn't close.

Wispr is $15/mo — $180/yr, forever. SpeakEasy is $99 once, and you bring your own OpenAI key, so dictation runs about $0.006/min. At typical usage that's roughly $46/year in usage instead of $180.

→ Pays for itself in ~9 months, then saves ~$130/year — every year.

I'll be straight: if you need 100+ languages or it on your phone, Wispr wins — buy Wispr. But if you're producing at a Windows desk and want your voice wired into your tools for pennies, it's not a contest.
