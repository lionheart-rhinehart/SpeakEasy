Straight answers

Doesn't Wispr support way more languages?

Yes — 100+ vs our ~13. If you work across many languages, Wispr is the better dictation engine and we'll say so. Most of us produce in English, where the difference is moot — and you gain a whole automation layer.

I need it on my phone or Mac.

Then Wispr's range fits you. SpeakEasy is a Windows power tool built for where marketing actually gets produced — your desk. It's not trying to be a phone app.

Is bringing my own API key a hassle?

Five minutes, once. That buys you a tool you own instead of a subscription you rent forever — and dictation drops to pennies. The calculator above shows the real total.

Is SpeakEasy private / offline?

Straight answer: transcription is cloud (OpenAI Whisper), same as Wispr. Neither is offline. What's different is you hold your own keys and there's no surveillance incident on our record.

Wispr's formatting looks cleaner.

Their automatic formatting is nice. Ours is on-demand through AI transform — and the same feature also rewrites, summarizes, and restyles on command, which dictation-only can't do.
